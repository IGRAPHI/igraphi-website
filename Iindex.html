How I Work

1. Strategic Discovery: Every engagement starts with understanding context — the organizational goals, the audiences, the constraints, and the stakes. I define a clear creative direction before any design work begins.

2. Intentional Design: I design visual systems that are clear, modern, and purposeful. Every choice — from typography to layout to color — serves the story the organization needs to tell.

3. Institutional-Grade Execution: I deliver production-ready assets built for real-world use: multi-language documents, scalable brand toolkits, event systems, and digital platforms. I collaborate closely with stakeholders, communications teams, and developers to ensure everything works at scale.

4. Refinement & Continuity: Design doesn't stop at delivery. I assess performance, gather feedback, and refine. Many of my client relationships span years because the work evolves with the mission.

The World Reconstruction Conference

About the Conference

The World Reconstruction Conference (WRC) is a premier global forum that assesses and shares experiences on disaster recovery and reconstruction. Since its inception in 2011, the conference has brought together international stakeholders — including governments, international organizations, NGOs, and the private sector — to advance the discourse on resilient recovery.

I designed a comprehensive brand identity that visually communicates complex themes of recovery, resilience, and long-term rebuilding on a global stage.

About GFDRR

The Global Facility for Disaster Reduction and Recovery (GFDRR) is a global partnership managed by the World Bank. It helps developing countries better understand and reduce their vulnerability to natural hazards and climate change.

Partnering with GFDRR required establishing a visual system that inspired credibility and fostered alignment among its diverse international partners, ensuring a cohesive and recognizable event identity.

GFDRR Partnership Days

About the Event

The GFDRR Partnership Days is a yearly global convening for partners under the World Bank. The event brings together a diverse group of international stakeholders, policymakers, and experts to align on a unified narrative of resilience and collaboration against natural hazards and climate change.

The Challenge & Outcome

Challenge: Aligning diverse stakeholders around a unified narrative required a comprehensive visual system across print, digital, and on-site assets — banners, reports, agendas, floor graphics, and email communications.

Outcome: I led the visual system design, enabling a multi-year, cohesive, and recognizable event identity that reinforced GFDRR's mission and dramatically improved engagement and clarity.
